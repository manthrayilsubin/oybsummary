Here's a summary of the sermon in 50 bulleted points:

**Theme: God's Provision for Holiness**

* This is the second of four studies on the beauty of holiness.
* The first study emphasized God's holiness and His demand for holiness in His people.
* God always provides the means for His people to meet His demands.
* This study focuses on God's provision for holiness.
* Holiness is a unique aspect of God's nature.
* God provides for us to partake in His total nature.
* Key Scripture: 2 Peter 1:2-4.
* Christian life is a life of multiplying grace and peace.
* Spiritual growth is essential for a genuine Christian experience.
* Knowing God and Jesus Christ is fundamental.
* 2 Peter 1:3 states God's divine power has given all things pertaining to life and godliness.
* Provision is received through acknowledging Jesus Christ.
* Unity in the church comes through acknowledging Christ, not doctrinal debates.
* Acknowledging Christ applies to salvation, healing, baptism in the Holy Spirit, and deliverance.
* 2 Peter 1:4 states provision is found in God's exceeding great and precious promises.
* Slogan: "The provision is in the promises."
* Appropriating promises results in partaking of the divine nature.
* Partaking of God's nature leads to escaping the world's corruption.
* Four key points from 2 Peter 1:3-4:
* Full provision already made.
* Provision comes through acknowledging Christ.
* Provision is in God's promises.
* Appropriating promises results in partaking of God's nature and escaping corruption.
* Illustration from Joshua 1:1-3:
* Old Covenant: Promised Land; New Covenant: Land of Promises.
* Joshua (Jesus) leads into inheritance.
* Legal possession vs. experiential possession.
* Moses' death parallels the death of the institutional church.
* Joshua 1:2-3 shows God's giving in present and past tense â€“ legal and experiential.
* Hebrews 12:10 emphasizes partaking in God's holiness.
* Seven provisions for holiness:
* Jesus Christ
* The Cross (sacrifice)
* The Holy Spirit
* The Blood of Jesus
* The Word of God
* Faith
* Works (actions expressing faith)
* Sanctification is in Christ Jesus (1 Corinthians 1:2, 30).
* The cross' sacrifice perfectly perfects those being sanctified (Hebrews 10:14).
* Perfect sacrifice vs. progressive appropriation.
* The Holy Spirit administers Christ's grace (1 Corinthians 6:11).
* God's choice for salvation is through sanctification of the Spirit and belief of the truth (2 Thessalonians 2:13, 1 Peter 1:2).
* The blood of Jesus separates us from our sinful past (1 Peter 1:2).
* Deliberate rejection of Christ's blood can result in loss of sanctification (Hebrews 10:29).
* Jesus' blood sanctifies (Hebrews 13:12).
* The Word of God sanctifies (John 17:17, 2 Thessalonians 2:13, Ephesians 5:26-27).
* The laver in the Old Testament represents the cleansing of the Word.
* Redemption through blood and cleansing through the Word (Exodus 30:18-21, Exodus 38:8).
* Three witnesses: blood, water (Word), Spirit (1 John 5:6).
* Faith is the means of appropriating God's provision.
* Faith without works is dead (James 2:26).
* Cleansing ourselves from filthiness of flesh and spirit (2 Corinthians 7:1).
* Working out our salvation with fear and trembling (Philippians 2:12-13).
