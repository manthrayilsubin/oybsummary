Here's a summary of the sermon in 50 bulleted points:

**I. Introduction & Defining Holiness:**

* Theme: The Beauty of Holiness (four-part series).
* Holiness is a major, unique biblical theme, neglected in recent decades.
* Some groups emphasize rules, lacking scriptural basis.
* Holiness is not about rule-keeping.
* Key interconnected Greek words: *hagios* (holy), saint, sanctification.
* "Saints" is the plural of "holy."
* "Sanctify" means "to make holy."
* "Sanctification" means "making holy."
* These words will be used interchangeably.
* Holiness is a unique aspect of God's nature.
* God's attributes (wisdom, knowledge, justice, power, love) have parallels in creation, but holiness does not.
* To understand holiness, one must know God.
* Agur (Proverbs 30:2-3) highlights the importance of knowing the holy.
* Lack of knowledge of the holy equates to brutishness.
* Revelation of God's holiness lifts man to a higher plane.

**II. God's Holiness Revealed in Scripture:**

* Isaiah 6: Vision of God's throne; seraphim (burning ones) worship.
* Seraphim's six wings: two for covering face, two for feet (worship), two for flying (service).
* "Holy, holy, holy," threefold for the Trinity.
* Isaiah's reaction: a sense of unworthiness.
* God's provision: a live coal (Holy Spirit) purifies Isaiah.
* God doesn't use volunteers; He calls the unqualified.
* Revelation 4: John's vision; the throne's prominence (14 mentions).
* Continual declaration of "Holy, holy, holy."
* Exodus 15:11: God's glory in holiness.
* Joshua 24:19: God's holiness and unforgiving nature.
* 1 Samuel 2: Hannah's song; God's uniqueness and holiness.
* Psalm 22:3: God enthroned upon the praises of Israel.
* Isaiah 57:15: God dwells with the contrite and humble.
* God's holiness provokes humility.
* Pride is a denial of God's holiness.

**III. God's Demand for Holiness in His People:**

* Leviticus emphasizes holiness (over 60 mentions).
* Leviticus 11:44-45; 19:2; 20:7, 26: God's holiness demands our holiness.
* Priesthood's role in teaching holiness.
* Exodus 19:6; Deuteronomy 14:2: God's people are holy, distinct, and peculiar.
* Deuteronomy 26:18-19: Holiness is linked to being exalted.
* 1 Peter 1:15-16; 2:9: Old Testament requirements apply to Christians.
* Revelation 1:5-6; 5:10: Believers are kings and priests.
* Functions of kings (rule) and priests (sacrifice, intercession).

**IV. New Testament Emphasis on Holiness:**

* 2 Corinthians 7:1: Cleansing from fleshly and spiritual filthiness; perfecting holiness.
* "Therefore": links to promises in 2 Corinthians 6.
* 1 Thessalonians 3:13: Unblameable holiness at Christ's return.
* Imminent expectation of Christ motivates holiness.
* 1 Thessalonians 4:3-4: Abstaining from fornication; possessing the body in holiness.
* 1 Thessalonians 5:23: Sanctification of spirit, soul, and body.
* Hebrews 12:10, 14: Discipline leads to partaking of God's holiness; holiness is essential for seeing God.
* Proverbs 4:18: The path of the righteous shines brighter.
* Holiness is progressive, not a static condition.

**V. Holiness and Beauty:**

* Holiness corresponds to natural beauty.
* Psalm 93:5; 96:9: Holiness beautifies God's house and worship.
* Psalm 110: God's people at the end times, "beauties of holiness."
* 1 Peter 3:3-5: The adornment of a meek and quiet spirit.


This detailed outline captures the essence of the sermon's message.
