Here's a summary of Jeremiah 31:27-32:44 in 50 bullets, with main points and sub-bullets:

**Jeremiah 31:27-34: New Covenant Promises**

- **Future Blessing and Reversal of Corruption (31:27-28)**
- The Lord promises to greatly increase the population and number of animals in Israel and Judah.
- The reversal of past disasters and destruction.

- **Personal Responsibility for Sin (31:29-30)**
- Each person will be held accountable for their own sins.
- Children will not suffer for their parents' sins.

- **New Covenant (31:31-34)**
- The Lord will make a new covenant with the people of Israel.
- This covenant will be internal, with the Lord's instructions written on their hearts.
- All people will know the Lord, and they will be forgiven and no longer remember their sins.

**Jeremiah 31:35-37: Unchangeable Covenant Maker**

- **Unchangeable Nature (31:35-36)**
- The Lord's unfailing love for His people is likened to the unchangeable laws of nature.
- He will not cast them away due to their evil deeds.

- **Unending Love (31:37)**
- Like the uncountable stars and vast oceans, so are His promises and unfailing love.

**Jeremiah 32:1-5: Jeremiah's Imprisonment and Prophecy to Zedekiah**

- **Jeremiah's Imprisonment (32:1-3)**
- Jeremiah is imprisoned in the courtyard of the royal palace due to his prophecies about Jerusalem's fall.

- **Prophecy to Zedekiah (32:4-5)**
- Jerusalem will fall to the Babylonians, and Zedekiah will be taken to Babylon.

**Jeremiah 32:6-15: Jeremiah Buys a Field**

- **The Lord's Message to Jeremiah (32:6-7)**
- Jeremiah's cousin Hanamel will ask him to buy his field in Anathoth.

- **Buying the Field (32:8-12)**
- Jeremiah buys the field in Anathoth for 17 pieces of silver, seals and weighs out the silver, and hands the deed to Baruch.

- **Preserving the Deed (32:13-15)**
- Jeremiah gives the deed to Baruch to put into a pottery jar to preserve it for a long time.
- People will again own property and buy and sell houses and vineyards in the land.

**Jeremiah 32:16-25: Jeremiah's Prayer**

- **Praise for the Lord's Power and Wisdom (32:16-21)**
- Jeremiah praises the Lord for His power and wisdom in creation and history.

- **Jeremiah's Confession (32:22-23)**
- Jeremiah confesses that the actions of the people deserve the coming judgment.

- **Request for Intervention (32:24-25)**
- Jeremiah asks the Lord to intervene and perform signs to show that He is the Lord of all the earth.

**Jeremiah 32:26-44: Prophecy about the Land's Future**

- **The Lord's Response to Jeremiah (32:26-30)**
- The Lord responds to Jeremiah, restating the coming judgment but also promising future restoration.

- **Future Blessings and Restoration (32:37-44)**
- Jerusalem will be rebuilt and never again captured or destroyed.
- The entire area will be holy to the Lord.
- The people will live in the land, build houses, plant vineyards, and enjoy peace and security.
